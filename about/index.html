About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in theoretical and computational statistical physics. I am interested in systems which display interesting collective phenomena, particularly glasses and systems undergoing self assembly. Most recently I have been using the swap Monte Carlo algorithm to explore glassy systems at temperatures and densities where it would not normally be possible to equilibrate them.

I currently work in the Laboratoire Charles Coulomb at the Université de Montpellier in France. I previously worked in the Condensed Matter Theory group at the University of Bath and before that I completed a PhD at the University of Manchester.
